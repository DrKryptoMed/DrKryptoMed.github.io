Overview

AuxiChron is an AI-powered healthcare platform designed for patients living with chronic conditions who need continuous, longitudinal care. It is not a symptom checker or a simple scheduling tool — it functions as a coordinated agentic system where every patient interaction is routed, analyzed, and acted on intelligently.

The system is built around a central Gemini-powered coordinator that classifies incoming inputs and dispatches them to one of four specialized clinical pathways. It surfaces insights to both patients and clinicians in real time, grounded in Nigerian clinical standards and designed to work across connectivity levels — from a browser to a low-resource WhatsApp bot.

Agentic gateway

The core of the system is a Gemini-powered coordinator that receives all patient inputs — text, voice notes, uploaded documents — and routes them to the appropriate clinical pathway. This is the agentic layer: it decides what kind of interaction is happening and delegates accordingly.

01

Clinical triage

A structured symptom-gathering flow anchored to the Nigerian Standard Treatment Guidelines (NSTG 2022). The system assesses urgency, assigns a priority score, and books the patient into the appropriate consultation tier — emergency, urgent, or routine.

Pathway 1 — Triage
02

Diagnostic summarization

An OCR + AI pipeline that ingests lab reports, biopsy results, and imaging summaries and converts them into plain-language summaries. Patients receive a readable interpretation; clinicians receive the structured output for their records.

Pathway 2 — Diagnostics
03

Pharmacy adherence tracking

A chronic medication refill tracker that monitors prescription timelines and flags "Adherence Gaps" when a patient goes more than 30 days without a documented refill. Triggers automated nudges and escalates to the clinician dashboard.

Pathway 3 — Adherence
04

Mental health sentiment monitoring

An NLP-driven module that continuously analyzes the sentiment of patient messages and clinical notes. Flags "Treatment Fatigue" or emotional distress signals, routing them to a clinician alert — ensuring mental health is treated as a clinical variable, not an afterthought.

Pathway 4 — Mental health

Design intent: Each pathway is specialized and purpose-built, but all share a unified data layer. A patient flagged for adherence gaps in Pathway 3 will have that context surface in the clinician's triage queue and in the mental health sentiment score — the system reasons holistically.

Interfaces

Provider command center

The clinician's interface is designed as a decision-support tool, not a scheduling list. It is built to surface what matters, ranked by clinical significance.

AI-ranked triage queue

A dynamic "Smart Waitlist" that ranks patients by a composite AI priority score — combining vitals severity, medication adherence gaps, and mental health sentiment flags — rather than simple arrival time. First-come-first-served is replaced by first-need-first-served.

Longitudinal vitals trends

Interactive 30-day trend charts for key vitals (blood pressure, glucose) layered against medication refill logs, making the correlation between adherence and clinical outcomes directly visible to the clinician.

AI clinical scribe

Transcribes live consultations in real time and auto-generates structured clinical notes in SOAP format (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan). Reduces documentation burden so the clinician can focus on the patient.

Human-in-the-loop controls

All AI-suggested treatment adjustments or referrals surface as actionable cards with Accept / Dismiss toggles. The clinician is always the final decision-maker. AI augments, not replaces.

Patient sovereign portal

The patient-facing interface is built around two principles: simplicity of access and ownership of data.

Health pulse dashboard

A minimalist home screen featuring a circular Adherence/Trust ring that visualizes the patient's current engagement score at a glance, and a one-tap button to begin a triage session.

Sovereign health vault

A longitudinal, encrypted record of all encounters, lab results, prescriptions, and AI summaries — owned by the patient, not the provider. Patients can share selected records with any clinician.

Multi-channel access

Patients interact via the web app or a low-resource WhatsApp bot. All interactions sync to the same Supabase backend in real time — ensuring the system works even on feature phones with limited data.

Telemedicine integration

Built-in video and audio consultation capabilities for virtual visits, with consultation fee payment scaffolded via Paystack and Flutterwave.

Clinical context

AuxiChron was built with the Nigerian clinical environment explicitly in mind — not as an afterthought. The triage logic is grounded in the Nigerian Standard Treatment Guidelines (NSTG 2022), the official clinical reference for Nigerian healthcare facilities.

This matters because most AI healthcare tools are trained on, and designed for, Western clinical workflows and demographics. AuxiChron takes a different approach: the priority scoring, drug adherence thresholds, and triage escalation criteria are all calibrated to the Nigerian context.

On model fairness: The AI-ranked triage queue is itself an equity intervention. By replacing first-come-first-served with a need-based priority score that factors in adherence, mental health, and vitals — the system structurally reduces the advantage of proximity and scheduling access. Bias is considered a clinical variable, not a technical footnote.

Who it's built for

Chronic disease patients — particularly those managing hypertension, diabetes, or other conditions requiring long-term medication adherence and monitoring. The system is designed to reduce the gap between clinic visits, not just facilitate them.

Clinicians in under-resourced settings — where patient loads are high, documentation time is scarce, and decision support is minimal. The AI scribe and triage queue are designed to reclaim clinician time, not add to it.
