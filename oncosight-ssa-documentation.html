01 — Problem Statement

The Clinical Crisis This Addresses

Breast and cervical cancer are among the leading causes of cancer mortality in Sub-Saharan African women. In Kenya — as across the region — the majority of cases are diagnosed at advanced stages, largely due to fragmented screening infrastructure, geographic barriers to oncology care, and persistently low health-seeking behaviour.

Existing AI tools for cancer triage are predominantly designed for high-resource Western healthcare systems with dense clinical data and reliable referral pathways. They fail to account for low-data environments, facility scarcity, transportation barriers, and the socio-economic factors that determine whether a referred patient ever receives care.

02 — System Architecture

Four Coordinated Agents

The system is designed as a multi-agent pipeline where each agent handles a distinct clinical task, outputs are passed downstream, and every decision is paired with an explainability layer accessible to clinicians and health workers.

🩺
AGENT 01

Cancer Risk Screener

Classifies patient risk level for breast and cervical cancer from demographic, reproductive, and clinical features. Produces a risk tier (low / moderate / high) with feature-level attribution.

03 — Explainability

Why Every Decision Must Be Auditable

In low-resource clinical settings, a black-box AI output is not just unhelpful — it is unsafe. Health workers need to understand why a patient is flagged high-risk before acting on it. Clinicians need to be able to challenge, override, and audit the system. This is a clinical imperative, not a technical nicety.

Each agent is paired with a specific explainability approach:

Method Applied To What It Answers
SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) Risk Screener, Compliance Agent Which features drove this prediction, and by how much?
LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations) Risk Screener How does the model behave around this specific patient's data?
Counterfactual Explanations Risk Screener, Compliance Agent What would need to change for the outcome to be different?
LLM Reasoning Trace Recommendation Agent Why was this referral plan generated? What clinical logic was applied?

06 — Research Contribution

What Makes This Novel

  • 01 First integrated pipeline combining cancer triage, facility mapping, referral recommendation, and health-seeking behaviour prediction into a single agentic system for the Sub-Saharan African context.
  • 02 Compliance modeling as a first-class citizen. Most clinical AI systems stop at risk classification. This system explicitly models whether a patient will follow through — and surfaces the structural barriers preventing care access.
  • 03 Explainability built for clinical trust in low-resource settings. SHAP, LIME, and counterfactual explanations are presented in plain language for health workers — not just for data scientists.
  • 04 Contextual grounding in African epidemiology. The system is trained and validated on Kenya DHS data, not imputed Western proxies. Cultural, geographic, and systemic barriers are modeled — not ignored.
  • 05 Health equity as a design constraint. Model fairness and demographic parity are evaluated explicitly across age, SES, and region — because biased clinical AI has measurably different costs in low-resource settings.
