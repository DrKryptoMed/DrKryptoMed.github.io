About Me

​​I am a Clinical and Public Health Data Scientist dedicated to transforming complex healthcare datasets into actionable clinical outcomes. By integrating medical domain expertise with advanced AI analytics, I aim to improve the healthcare landscape through a precision-first lens.

Currently a medical student (MBBS) with a strong foundation in Human Anatomy (BSc), Public Health (MSc), and Computer Science, I bridge the gap between clinical observation and the computational power of data science. This dual perspective allows me to move beyond raw data, generating the predictive insights necessary to improve patient survival rates and also optimize population health strategies.

My work focuses on the complexities of oncological outcomes—ranging from cancer epidemiology and genomics to risk profiling and treatment response modeling. While my primary focus is oncology, my methodology is designed to be scalable across the entire chronic disease landscape.

My mission is to ensure that data is used to drive precision medicine and effective, personalized health responses leveraging responsible Artificial Intelligence.

This portfolio showcases my projects at this intersection.
