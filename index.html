About Me

​​I don't just analyze healthcare data, I build the systems that act on it.

I'm Ibrahim Mikail, a 3rd-year medical student with an MSc in Public Health and a full professional practice in Data Science, AI/ML, and MLOps. That combination is rare, and I use it deliberately, to build healthcare AI that is both technically rigorous and clinically grounded.

My work has evolved from classical data analysis and predictive modeling into designing and deploying end-to-end agentic AI systems for healthcare including LLM-powered clinical tools, RAG pipelines for medical knowledge retrieval, ML inference pipelines with MLOps infrastructure, and clinical decision support applications built fullstack from model to UI.

What sets my perspective apart is that I understand the data and the disease. I can read an ICD-10 code, interpret a pathology report, and also architect the pipeline that processes it at scale. I think about model fairness not as a checkbox, but as a clinical imperative because I've seen what biased tools cost patients.

My current focus areas include vascular disease AI, oncological outcome modeling, health equity in algorithmic systems, and agentic workflows for clinical settings. I build with Python, deploy on cloud infrastructure, and ship production grade applications and not just notebooks.

I'm not waiting to finish medical school to do meaningful work. I'm doing it now.
