I'm a medical student with a background in Public Health(MSc) and a full professional practice in Data Science, AI/ML, and MLOps. That combination is rare, and I use it deliberately — to build healthcare AI that is both technically rigorous and clinically grounded.

My work has evolved from classical data analysis into designing and deploying end-to-end agentic AI systems: LLM-powered clinical tools, RAG pipelines for medical knowledge retrieval, ML inference pipelines with MLOps infrastructure, and clinical decision support applications built fullstack from model to UI.

What sets my perspective apart is that I understand the data and the disease. I can read an ICD-10 code, interpret a pathology report, and architect the pipeline that processes it at scale. I think about model fairness not as a checkbox, but as a clinical imperative — because I've seen what biased tools cost patients.
